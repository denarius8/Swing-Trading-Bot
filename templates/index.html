Position Size Calculator
What this does: Tells you exactly how many shares to buy so that if your stop loss gets hit, you only lose a fixed % of your account — never more. This is the #1 rule professional traders use to survive losing streaks.

How to use this:
1. Account $ — Enter your total available trading capital (e.g. $10,000)
2. Entry $ — The price you plan to enter the trade at. Use the current price or your limit order price
3. Stop $ — Your invalidation level. Where the trade thesis is wrong. For longs: below entry. For shorts: above entry. Use a key support/resistance level, or the ATR-based stop from the SPX Options tab
4. Risk % — The maximum percentage of your account you're willing to lose on this single trade:
1% = Conservative — survive 100 consecutive losses. Best for learning or small accounts
2% = Standard — the most common professional rule. Survive 50 consecutive losses
3-5% = Aggressive — only for high-conviction trades. A 10-trade losing streak costs 30-50% of account

Example: $10K account, entering PEP at $150 with a $140 stop at 2% risk →
Risk amount = $10,000 × 2% = $200
Risk per share = $150 − $140 = $10
Shares to buy = $200 ÷ $10 = 20 shares ($3,000 position)
If PEP hits $140 stop → you lose exactly $200 (2%). Account survives.
